My bio

I was born in Rhode Island in 1983 but moved to California before I was even a year old. Spent the rest of my life there until I joined the Army in 2002. In 2005 I was deployed to Iraq as a Combat Medic in support of the 1-30th Infantry. I deployed again in 2009 as a Combat Medic with the 103rd Field Artillary. When I return to Rhode Island I was transfered to the MedDet where I took over as the clinical NCOIC. I ran the clinic for close to 8 years under the guidence of the DSS and the unit commander and first Sergeant. After 17 years of service, I was medically retired from the Armed Forces as a Sergeant First Class (SFC/E7).

In 2007 I decided to use my GI bill to attend college. I started at RIC working on my nursing degree, and I eventually decided to switch my major to biology. After my second deployment I went back to school but this time I went to CCRI and earned my Associates in Business and then continued on to earn an Associates in Computer Studies and Information Processing. During this time I fell in love and got married to my wonderful wife, but after less then a year of marriage my step-daughter was diagnosed with ALL and I had to take a break from my schooling to help my family. After my daughter was released from Hasboro Children's Hospital, and I was retired from the military, I decided to attend NEIT to pursue my bachelor's degree in Video Game Development and Simulation Programming.
